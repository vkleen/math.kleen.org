published: 2018-02-02

Here is a formula from vector calculus:
$$
\displaystyle
\int_{S^1\times D^2}\<j, E> \dd^3 x = \int_{D^2} \<j, \dd A> \int_{S^1} \<E, \dd\ell>
$$
for $j, E\colon S^1\times D^2\to \RR^3$ vector fields with $\div{j} = 0$ and $\curl{E} = 0$.
